Books



"The Architect's Apprentice" follows the journey of Jahan, a young boy who becomes the apprentice to the famous architect Sinan in 16th century Istanbul. Amidst the backdrop of the Ottoman Empire, Jahan navigates the complexities of life, love, and ambition while learning the art of architecture.

My favorite quote from the book: “For apprentices everywhere - no one told us that love was the hardest craft to master”

"Lust for Life" by Irving Stone is a biographical novel that delves deep into the tumultuous life of Vincent van Gogh. The novel provides a poignant exploration of van Gogh's inner world and the profound impact of his mental health challenges on his art.

My favorite quote from the book: "A person may paint or talk about painting but he cannot do both at the same time."

"The Phantom Tollbooth" follows the journey of a young boy named Milo who receives a mysterious tollbooth. He finds himself in a magical land called the Kingdom of Wisdom. He encounters quirky characters, faces challenges that test his wit, and learns valuable lessons about the power of curiosity and imagination.

My favorite quote from the book: "The most important reason for going from one place to another is to see what's in between."

"If Beale Street Could Talk" follows the story of Tish, a young African-American woman in 1970s Harlem, as she navigates the challenges of love and justice when her fiancé, Fonny, is falsely accused of a crime. Determined to prove his innocence, Tish and her family strive against systemic racism and prejudice to secure Fonny's freedom before their baby is born.

My favorite quote from the book: “...love brought you here. If you trusted love this far, don't panic now.”

"The Namesake" follows the journey of Gogol Ganguli, the son of Bengali immigrants, as he navigates the complexities of identity and cultural belonging in America. Born with a name that bridges two worlds but feeling disconnected from both, Gogol embarks on a quest for self-discovery amidst family expectations and personal desires.

My favorite quote from the book: "You remind me of everything that followed."
